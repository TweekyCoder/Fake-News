How To Identify?
Identification
Though it looks hard, but there are many ways of identifying fake/fraud news. You just need to be extra attentive and these 10 basic rules will help you to identify them.
The 9 Steps Method
  1. Do A Visual Assessment - Grade the overall design. Fake news sites often look misleading, having lots of annoying pop-up ads, and uses edited or copied images.
  2. Check the media outlet URL - Is it a misleading website? Has the URL been altered? Example: www.THEWASHINGTONPOST.com could be: www.THEWASHINGT0NPOST.com
  3. Spelling, Grammar, and Punctuation - Has the author misspelt any words? If yes, it’s likely to be fake as most news articles go through thorough editing before being published.
  4. The Author - Fake media outlets often don’t have names. Check out who wrote the news article and if he or she is a journalist or not.
  5. What is the Main Point - What is the author’s main point? This may be misleading because of emotional biasness and liking or disliking for particular person or organization.
  6. Analyse Sources and Quotes - Check the sources and who wrote the quotes. Fake news articles mostly contain anonymous quotes.
  7. Find other resources to double check – Finally you can check www.factcheck.org, www.snopes.com, www.politifact.com websites as – these are fact checking websites as most likely they would have written about the news.
